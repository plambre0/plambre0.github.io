Recent Projects

2026

Estimating Effect of Violent Crime Rates on PTSD Prevalence in Chicago

Used neighborhood level Mental Health America survey data and Chicago socioeconomic statistics to estimate the total effects of violent crime rate on PTSD prevalence controlling for confounding factors using spatial statistics, GLM, SEM, and Bayesian methods.

2025

An Evaluation of the Effectiveness of Lead Exposure Regulations on Kidney Health

Used NHANES data to evaluate the effectiveness of lead exposure regulations. Built mediation models to test the hypothesis that these policies reduced blood lead levels and prevelance of kidney damage in the US poplation adjusting for confounders and survey design effects.

2025-2026

shellFisk: A High-Performance Statistical Computing Environment

Designed a high-performance statistical programming language and analysis environment in C++ supporting GLM, PCA, Nonparametric Tests (ANOVA, Wilcoxon, Kruskal-Wallis), Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis, and data wrangling. Supports undo-redo functionality with an input stack, outputting sessions, CSV formatted datasets, and JSON format model outputs. Methods using linear algebra were implemented using the Eigen library.

About Me

I am a statistical analyst who thrives on leveraging information to drive complex problem solving and decision-making. Through my work in statistical computing, epidemiology, and computational biology, I have developed a diverse skill set in statistical inference, modeling, risk analysis, large scale data processing, visualization, and communication skills.

This June, I will receive my BS in Mathematics and Computer Science from DePaul University where I minored in Data Science and concentrated my studies on statistical methods and data analysis including graduate coursework in Stochastic Processes, Advanced Statistical Computing, and Time Series Analysis.

I am an alumni of DePaul's Combined and Computational Bioinformatics Lab. During my time there, I co-authored a research paper that is currently under ICBCB's peer review process where we developed machine learning methods to analyze gene expression patterns in neural spatial transcriptomics data. During my studies at DePaul, I also gained experience at Indiana University Bloomington's Khomtchouk Lab where I designed and implemented statistical studies focusing on the causes and progression of cardiometabolic diseases.

My professional interests include actuarial science, economics, and public health. My academic interests include causal inference, bayesian statistics, and the development of interpretable statistical and machine learning models to drive informed decision-making regarding complex systems.
